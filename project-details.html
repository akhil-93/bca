Cyber-Threat Intelligence

Cybersecurity is being transformed from localized mitigation to a comprehensive notion of cyber threat intelligence that encompasses information on Threat Vectors (the mechanisms by which the infrastructure is compromised), Threat Actors (States, Criminal Organizations, Hacktivists, etc.), Indicators of Compromise, and Actionable Advice- information that must be analyzed after collection and enrichment. We develop a Network-centric framework as the central component of Cyber Threat Intelligence in the context of the NIST SP800-171. The application of Network Science with its use of data mining, graph theory, statistical mechanics, and other advanced analytical tools provides good underpinning for the understanding network behavior and its implications for threat intelligence.(Northrop Grumman Corp.)

IoT-based Home Healthcare Automation

T he Internet-of-Things (IoT) promises to handle the challenges of ageing populations through the delivery of high-quality in-home healthcare. However, much of the existing technology is being implemented without consideration of the business model, device and service integration and the information architecture. These considerations are applied to the design, development, and deployment of a in-home IoT based system, the iCare System.(Kerala Social Service Fund)
